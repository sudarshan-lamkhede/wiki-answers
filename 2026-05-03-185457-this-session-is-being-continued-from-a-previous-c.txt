⏺ Looks clean. A few design notes on what was done:

  - Stats dataclass (lines 62–67): accumulates tool_calls, input_tokens,
  output_tokens, and a list of ttft_ms samples across the entire session.
  - _classify_question (lines 109–126): uses time.perf_counter() around
  messages.parse() as TTFT — since the structured-output endpoint doesn't expose
   a streaming interface, wall-clock latency is the right proxy for a 64-token
  response.
  - _call_api (lines 135–178): switched from messages.create() to
  messages.stream(). It records TTFT on the first content_block_delta event
  (which fires whether Claude is generating text or a tool-call JSON), then
  calls stream.get_final_message() to get usage and the full content. Tool
  invocations are incremented into stats.tool_calls here (distinct from the
  local tool_calls_used cap counter).
  - _print_stats (lines 201–210): called after the main loop exits (both /end
  and EOF), never on sys.exit(1) error paths.

  {QuestionID}-{row_index} (index ensures uniqueness when a question has
